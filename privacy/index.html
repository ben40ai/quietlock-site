Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 11, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Quiet Lock (“Quiet Lock,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) collects, uses, discloses, and otherwise processes information when you use our mobile application (“App”) and related services we offer through the App (collectively, the “Service”). The operator of the Service is the developer entity identified on the App’s page in the Apple App Store (the “Developer”).

By using the Service, you agree to this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the Service. Please also read our Terms of Service, which govern use of the App.

Summary. Quiet Lock is designed so that core features work primarily on your device. We do not require you to create an account to use the core Service. We do not sell your personal information. We do not use your prayer content, journaling text, or similar reflections for advertising. If we enable product analytics or cloud features in the future, we will describe them here and in the App Store privacy details, and we will obtain consent where required by law.

1. Scope

This policy applies to the Service. It does not apply to third-party websites, apps, or services that may be linked from the Service or to Apple Inc.’s processing of information through iOS, Screen Time, the App Store, or system permissions (see Section 8).

2. Information we collect

Depending on how you use the Service, we may process the categories below. Unless stated otherwise, data described in Sections 2.1–2.4 is stored locally on your device using Apple’s on-device storage frameworks (for example, SwiftData and app storage). We do not transmit that local app data to our servers in the current core product design, except if you contact us by email or if you use optional features we describe as network-connected.

2.1 Information you provide

  • Profile and preferences. Information you enter in onboarding or settings, such as a display name, age range, optional gender selection, spiritual tradition or exploration preferences, mood selections, and optional time-of-day preferences for prayer.
  • User content. Text you add in journaling or notes features, titles or descriptions you attach to memory or timeline entries, and similar content you choose to save in the App.
  • Communications. If you email us at support or privacy addresses, we receive your email address, message contents, and metadata typical of email (such as timestamps).
  • Screen Time selections. When you use Apple’s Family Controls / Screen Time APIs to choose apps or categories to limit during a prayer session, Apple processes those selections under Apple’s policies. The App stores related configuration and session state on-device (and, where applicable, in an App Group container shared with our app extension) to enforce your choices.

Screen Time shielding. Although Quiet Lock uses Apple’s Screen Time–related APIs to shield apps or categories you select, the Developer does not have access to, and the App does not collect, any information regarding the specific content or user activity within the apps chosen for shielding. Enforcement relies on Apple’s system frameworks and tokens as designed by Apple.

2.2 Automatically processed information (on-device)

  • Service diagnostics on-device. Settings such as audio preferences, subscription state placeholders, prayer streak or engagement counters, and similar operational state needed to run the App.
  • Extension / shield state. Minimal technical state used by our Screen Time shield extension to coordinate temporary restrictions you have enabled (for example, timing and confirmation flows).

2.3 Product analytics (optional)

We may use an analytics provider to understand aggregate usage (for example, onboarding steps completed, screen views, tab selections, coarse subscription status, and high-level product events). Our design goal is to avoid sending prayer text, journal contents, or fine-grained location.

If analytics are enabled in your build:

  • We may process coarse regional information (such as country or broad region) derived primarily from your device’s locale or region settings. If product analytics are enabled, your analytics vendor may also infer coarse geography (for example, country or, in some cases, city-level) from network signals such as IP address processed on the vendor’s systems. The App does not request continuous precise GPS or otherwise collect precise location coordinates for analytics.
  • We may process coarse age band and subscription state derived from in-app profile and entitlement state.
  • We do not intend to collect advertising identifiers for cross‑app tracking as part of Quiet Lock’s core offering.

In the European Economic Area, United Kingdom, and Switzerland, we will activate non-essential analytics only where permitted by law (for example, after consent where consent is required). Analytics vendors act as our processors under documented terms.

2.4 Purchases

If you purchase a subscription or other paid feature through the App Store, Apple processes payment and provides us limited information (such as subscription status indicators) as described in Apple’s terms and documentation. We do not receive your full payment card number.

2.5 No intentional collection from children under 13

The Service is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. See Section 9.

3. How we use information

We use information to:

  • Provide, maintain, and improve the Service, including prayer sessions, optional app limiting, journaling, and personalization you request.
  • Remember your preferences and on-device history you choose to store.
  • Coordinate between the main App and our shield extension when you enable Screen Time–based features.
  • Provide customer support and respond to inquiries.
  • Protect the security and integrity of the Service, detect abuse, and comply with law.
  • Understand product usage in aggregate, if and when analytics are enabled consistent with this policy and your choices where required.
  • Manage subscriptions and entitlements consistent with App Store rules.

We do not use your spiritual reflections or journal entries to train generalized public artificial intelligence models. If that ever changes, we will obtain any legally required consent and update this policy materially in advance.

4. Legal bases (EEA / UK / CH)

Where the GDPR or similar laws apply, we rely on one or more of the following:

  • Contract — processing necessary to provide the Service you request.
  • Legitimate interests — securing the Service, understanding aggregate usage (where not overridden by your rights), and improving reliability, subject to balancing tests.
  • Consent — where required for optional analytics or marketing communications (we do not use marketing email unless you opt in).
  • Legal obligation — where we must retain or disclose information to comply with law.

5. Sharing and disclosure

We share information only as described below:

  • Service providers (processors). Hosting, analytics, crash reporting, or customer support tools that process data on our instructions under contracts.
  • Apple. The App is distributed via Apple’s App Store; Apple collects information as described in Apple’s privacy policy and the privacy labels shown on our App Store listing.
  • Legal and safety. We may disclose information if we believe in good faith that disclosure is required by law, regulation, legal process, or governmental request, or to protect rights, safety, and security of users, the public, or Quiet Lock.
  • Business transfers. If we are involved in a merger, acquisition, or asset sale, information may be transferred as part of that transaction subject to standard safeguards and notice where required.

We do not sell personal information as defined under U.S. state privacy laws, and we do not share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising as a “sale” or “sharing” absent a future feature that would require updated notice and, where applicable, opt-out or consent. We do not handle your prayer, journal, or similar on-device content for commercial sale or advertising profiling.

6. Retention

On-device data remains until you delete it within the App or delete the App from your device. Email support communications are retained only as long as needed to resolve your request and for legitimate business or legal record-keeping. Analytics events are retained according to our vendor settings (typically months) in aggregated or pseudonymous form where used.

7. Security

We use reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational measures appropriate to the nature of the Service. Because the Service stores meaningful content on your device, you should protect device access with a passcode or biometric and keep your operating system updated. No method of storage or transmission is completely secure.

8. Apple, Screen Time, and permissions

Certain features rely on Apple frameworks and system permissions (for example, Family Controls / Screen Time and optional notifications). Your use of those frameworks is also subject to Apple’s terms and privacy policy. Apple’s systems may impose technical or policy limitations that affect how or whether blocking features operate. Quiet Lock is not endorsed by or affiliated with Apple.

9. Children’s privacy

The Service is not intended for children under 13. If you believe we have inadvertently collected personal information from a child under 13, contact us at privacy@quietlock.app and we will take appropriate steps to delete it.

Users between 13 and the age of majority must have parental or guardian permission where required by local law.

10. International and transfers

Most personal data described in Sections 2.1–2.4 stays on your device; we do not receive the content of your local journal or prayer flow unless you send it to us (for example by email). If you email support or privacy addresses, we receive that correspondence where our mail systems operate. If we use analytics, hosting, or other processors, they may process limited technical or account-related data in India or other countries. Where personal data is transferred across borders, we comply with applicable law, including notice, consent, or approved transfer mechanisms where required.

11. Your privacy rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, port, or restrict certain processing, and to object to certain processing or profiling. You may also have the right to withdraw consent where processing was consent-based.

  • EEA / UK / CH: You may lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority.
  • India: If you are in India, you may have rights under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 and related rules (for example, to access, correct, or erase personal data we hold, to withdraw consent where processing is consent-based, or to escalate grievances). This typically covers information such as email or support messages and any personal data processed through optional analytics—not prayer or journal text that never leaves your device. Contact privacy@quietlock.app.
  • California and other U.S. states: Residents may have additional rights under state privacy laws (for example, rights to know, delete, and correct personal information, and to opt out of certain sales/sharing). Quiet Lock does not currently “sell” or “share” personal information as those terms are commonly defined; if that changes, we will provide a clear method to exercise opt-out rights.

To exercise rights, contact privacy@quietlock.app. We may need to verify your request consistent with applicable law. You may designate an authorized agent where permitted.

Apple App Store purchases: Many billing and receipt questions are best directed to Apple Support in addition to contacting us.

12. California “Shine the Light”

California residents may request information about disclosure of personal information to third parties for their direct marketing purposes. We do not disclose personal information to third parties for their direct marketing purposes as defined by that law.

13. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will post the updated version here and revise the “Last updated” date. If changes are material, we will provide additional notice as required by law (for example, an in-app notice or email where appropriate).
